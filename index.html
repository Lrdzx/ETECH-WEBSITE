BOOLEAN OPERATORS

What is a Boolean Operator?

Boolean operators are words or symbols used to combine search terms in databases and search engines to narrow or broaden search results. The most common operators are AND, OR, and NOT.

It’s based on Boolean logic, a form of algebra developed by George Boole, where values are either true or false.

Advantage of Using Boolean Operators

Boolean operators help make searches more precise by narrowing or broadening results as needed. They save time by filtering out unrelated information and allow flexible searching using combinations like AND, OR, and NOT.

Disadvantage of Using Boolean Operators

Using Boolean operators incorrectly can lead to poor or no results. Overusing NOT may remove valuable information, while excessive use of AND or OR can make results too narrow or too broad. Different databases or search engines may interpret these operators differently.

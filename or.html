What is the "OR" Operator?

The OR operator is a logical operator that returns true if at least one of the conditions it compares is true, and false only if all conditions are false.

Purpose of the "OR" Operator

The OR operator allows flexibility in logic or searches by accepting results that satisfy any one of multiple conditions. It broadens possibilities instead of limiting them to a single match.

Examples

Programming: if age < 18 OR age > 60 → Gives a discount if the person is either younger than 18 or older than 60.

Search Engine: cats OR dogs → Shows results containing cats, dogs, or both.

Everyday Logic: "We can have pizza OR burgers for dinner" → You’ll be satisfied if either pizza or burgers are available.

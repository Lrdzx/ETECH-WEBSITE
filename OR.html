What is the "OR" Operator?

The OR operator is a logical operator that returns true if at least one of the conditions it compares is true, and false only if all conditions are false. In Boolean logic, it is typically represented by the symbol || or the word OR. It is often used in decision-making and search queries to broaden results by including items that meet any of the given conditions.

Purpose of the "OR" Operator

The purpose of the OR operator is to allow flexibility in logic or searches by accepting results that satisfy any one of multiple conditions. It helps broaden possibilities instead of limiting them to a single match. This makes it useful in programming, decision-making, and search queries where multiple acceptable outcomes exist.

Examples

Programming: if age < 18 or age > 60: print("Discount applies") → Gives a discount if the person is either younger than 18 or older than 60.

Search engine: Searching for cats OR dogs will show results containing cats, dogs, or both.

Everyday logic: "We can have pizza or burgers for dinner" → You’ll be satisfied if either pizza or burgers are available.
